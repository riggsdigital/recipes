Baked Meatballs with Marinara and Mozzarella

Baked meatballs simmered in marinara and topped with melted mozzarella — straightforward ingredients, no fillers.

−
4 servings
+
⏰ 15 min prep · 25 min cook

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lb lean ground beef (or ground turkey)
  • 0.33 cup grated parmesan cheese
  • 0.33 cup plain breadcrumbs
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • 0.75 tsp salt
  • 0.25 tsp black pepper
  • 24 fl oz marinara sauce (jarred or homemade)
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 2 tbsp fresh basil leaves, torn (optional)

Steps

  1. 1
    Preheat oven

    Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment or foil.

  2. 2
    Mix meatball mixture

    In a large bowl, combine the ground beef, parmesan, breadcrumbs, egg, garlic, parsley, salt, and pepper. Mix gently with your hands until just combined — don't overwork it.

  3. 3
    Shape meatballs

    Roll the mixture into 16 golf-ball-sized meatballs and place them on the prepared baking sheet, spaced apart.

  4. 4
    Bake meatballs

    Bake until browned and mostly cooked through (they'll finish cooking in the sauce).

    ⏰ 15:00
  5. 5
    Add meatballs to marinara

    Warm the marinara sauce in a large oven-safe skillet or baking dish over medium heat. Add the baked meatballs and spoon sauce over them.

  6. 6
    Top with mozzarella and melt

    Sprinkle mozzarella evenly over the meatballs and sauce. Transfer to the oven (or broiler) until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

    ⏰ 8:00
  7. 7
    Garnish and serve

    Remove from oven, scatter torn basil on top, and serve.

Notes

Serve over zucchini noodles or a small portion of pasta depending on your carb goals for the day — the meatballs and sauce themselves are low-carb. Ground turkey works well as a leaner swap for the beef. Leftovers keep well for lunches.
